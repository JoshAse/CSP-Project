Physics

Thermodynamics, gas expansion, and fluid behavior

Heat Transfer

Heat flows from warmer water to colder dry ice due to the temperature difference between them. This transfer occurs through conduction and convection, increasing the rate of sublimation of solid CO₂.

Gas Expansion and Pressure

As dry ice sublimates, it produces large volumes of carbon dioxide gas. According to gas laws, this rapid increase in gas volume raises pressure inside the flexible container, leading to a sudden outward release.

Fun Corner

From a physics perspective, CryoBlast relies on heat transfer and gas expansion. Thermal energy flows from the warmer water to the colder dry ice due to the temperature difference. This energy transfer accelerates sublimation, increasing the kinetic energy of CO₂ molecules. As the solid CO₂ becomes gas, its volume expands significantly according to gas behavior principles. In a flexible container, this expansion leads to a rapid increase in internal pressure. When the gas is released, CO₂ disperses outward following fluid dynamics principles. Because carbon dioxide is denser than air, it spreads along the base of the flame, effectively displacing oxygen and reducing the flame’s temperature.

Fluid Dynamics and Cooling

Carbon dioxide is denser than air, allowing it to spread along the base of the flame and displace oxygen efficiently. The endothermic sublimation process also absorbs heat, lowering the combustion temperature.

Physics — Explained + Fun

From a physics perspective, CryoBlast relies on rapid heat transfer from water to dry ice, triggering sublimation. The resulting CO₂ gas expands rapidly, increasing pressure inside the flexible container until it is released outward.

Because CO₂ is denser than air, it spreads along the base of the flame, displacing oxygen efficiently. At the same time, the endothermic sublimation process absorbs thermal energy, lowering the temperature of the combustion zone.

Fun Physics Moment

“Fire needs oxygen. CO₂ said: not today.”

This is the same reason CO₂ fire extinguishers are effective in real-world fire suppression systems.
